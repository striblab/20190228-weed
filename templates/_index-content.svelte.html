As recreational marijuana gains legal acceptance throughout the nation state-by-state, Minnesota lawmakers are mulling the drug's future.

Gov. Tim Walz has expressed openness to legalizing marijuana for recreational use, following the lead of states like Colorado, Washington, Oregon, California and others.

The state of marijuana in Minnesota is complex and contradictory. On one hand, cannabis has been decriminalized for small doses in Minnesota since the 1970s and available as a treatment for terminal medical conditions since 2015. On the other hand, large racial disparities in enforcement persist statewide and the medical availability of marijuana-based treatments is among the country's most restrictive.
